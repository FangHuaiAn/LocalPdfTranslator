DURING THE FIRST SIX CENTURIES AFTER the birth of Christ, the geopolitical configuration of the Middle East stayed relatively stable, with the political landscape dominated by two huge empires. 在基督誕生後的頭六個世紀期間，中東的地緣政治格局相對穩定，政治格局由兩個龐大的帝國主導。

In the west, the Mediterranean littoral (roughly, modern Israel, Lebanon, and Syria) was part of the Roman Empire and then of its successor, the Byzantine Empire. 在西面，地中海沿岸（大致相當於現代的以色列、黎巴隆和敘利亞）是羅馬帝國的一部分，後來又成為其繼任者拜占庭帝國。

Until the early third century, the territory east of the Euphrates belonged to the Parthians; after that date, to the successor state of Sassanian Persia. 直到第三世紀初期，幼發拉底河以東的地區屬於波斯帝國；在那之後，則歸於薩珊王朝（波斯）。

Between the two imperial frontiers was wedged a huge area of dry steppe and desert, which extends south into the Arabian Peninsula. 兩大帝國之間的邊界之間，夾雜著一個巨大的乾燥草原和沙漠地區，延伸到阿拉伯半島南部。

The northern part of this arid zone is called the Syrian desert, but there is no physical barrier between it and the Arabian desert to the south. 這個乾燥地區的北部稱為敘利亞沙漠，但它與南部的阿拉伯沙漠之間沒有物理屏障。

The people who inhabited this region then (and still do so now) were the Arabs. 那時（以及現在）居住在這個地區的人是阿拉伯人。

The main adaptation that allowed the Arabs to live in the desert was domestication of the camel and the invention of the camel riding saddle. 阿拉伯人能夠在沙漠中生存的主要適應是馴養駱駝和發明騎駱駝的鞍子。

Camels could survive and thrive in areas where sheep and goats could not. 駱駝可以在羊和山羊無法生存的地方存活並繁榮。

They provided their owners with milk, meat, leather, and wool. 他們為他們的擁有者提供了牛奶、肉類、皮革和羊毛。

But their most important advantage over other domesticated animals was their ability to carry people and goods through waterless wastes of Syria and Arabia. 但它們在運載來自敘利亞和阿拉伯的水資源稀缺地區的人和貨物方面的優勢，遠勝於其他家養動物。

The camel was the critical “technology” that allowed the Arabs to expand over the whole Syrian and Arabian deserts during the first millennium B.C. 駱駝是使阿拉伯人在公元前一千年的第一千年期間能夠在整個敘利亞和阿拉伯沙漠中擴張的關鍵“技術”。

Not all Arabs were nomadic pastoralists. Not all Arabs were nomadic pastoralists. 並非所有阿拉伯人都是遊牧放牧者。

Some made a living from long-distance trade. 有些人從遠距離貿易中獲得生計。 (Several lucrative caravan routes ran through the desert.) （多條有利可圖的商隊路線穿過沙漠。）

Others penetrated into the borderlands of the Roman and Persian empires, and switched to a semi-nomadic way of life, or even settled down to agriculture. 另一些人滲透到羅馬和波斯帝國的邊境地區，轉而採行半遊牧的生活方式，甚至定居下來進行農業。 (Agriculture was also practiced in the oases of the Arabian Peninsula.) （在阿拉伯半島的綠洲中也實踐了農業。）

Finally, a number of cities in the Fertile Crescent, such as Palmyra, Edessa, and Hira, were ruled by Arab dynasties that arose in the Ibn Khaldunian fashion. 最終，像巴勒摩、以撒和希拉這樣的肥沃地區城市（如中東）由在伊本·卡爾둔式下興起的阿拉伯王朝統治。
