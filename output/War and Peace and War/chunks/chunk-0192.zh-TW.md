## 文檔路徑：Document

ONE OF THE MOST STRIKING historical trends in France during the twelfth and thirteenth centuries was massive population growth. 在 twelfth 和 thirteenth 個世紀中，法國最引人注目的歷史趨勢是人口的巨大增長。

In 1100, about six million people inhabited the territory within the modern national borders of France. 1100 年時，約有六百萬人在法國現代國家邊界內的一片區域居住。

By 1300, the population more than tripled and reached a level between 20 and 22 million.  到 1300 年，人口超過三倍，達到 20 到 22 百萬的水平。

The rest of Western Europe experienced a similar population buildup. 西歐的其他地區也經歷了類似的人口增長。

We are on firm ground when it comes to tracing the dynamics of the population of England.  我們在追蹤英格蘭人口動態方面擁有堅實的基礎。

In 1086, William the Conqueror, who wanted to know just how many new subjects he had acquired by his conquest of England, conducted a massive census whose results were preserved in the Domesday Book. 1086 年，威廉征服者，他想知道他在征服英格蘭時獲得了多少新臣民，進行了一次大規模人口普查，其結果被保存於《都城典籍》。

Using this information, modern historians estimate that there were around two million people in England at the end of the eleventh century.  利用這些信息，現代歷史學家估計 11世紀末英格蘭的人口約為兩百萬。

Two centuries later, there were close to six million.  兩個世紀 спустя，人口接近六百萬。

The population increase put the productive means of the medieval society under a collossal strain. 人口增長對中世紀社會的生產手段造成了巨大的壓力。

All land that could be cultivated was turned into fields.  所有可耕種的地都被改為田地。

In the process, more than 30 million acres of forests—one quarter of the modern area of France—were destroyed to make room for agriculture.  在這個過程中，超過 30 百萬英畝的森林——佔現代法國面積四分之一——被摧毀以供農業使用。

Land was worked more intensively by shifting from the two-field to the three-field system.  土地通過從兩種田地制轉變為三種田地制進行更集約性地開墾。

Instead of letting land rest every other year, each field was cultivated every two years out of three.  而不是讓土地每隔一年休息一次，每個田地每年耕作三分之二的年份。

As a result of increases in cultivated area and the switch to the three-field system, the amount of food produced in France probably doubled during the twelfth and thirteenth centuries.  由於耕種面積增加和轉變為三種田地制，法國在 twelfth 和 thirteenth 個世紀中的食物產量可能翻倍。

But the number of mouths to be fed increased threefold, with the inevitable result that the per-capita food consumption declined.  但要餵養的人口增加了三倍，不可避免的是，人均糧食消費量下降了。

In An Essay on the Principle of Population, published in 1798, the English economist Thomas Robert Malthus argued that when population increases beyond the means of subsistence food prices increase, real wages (that is, wages expressed in terms of purchasing power) decline, and per-capita consumption, especially among the poorer strata, drops.  在 1798 年出版的《關於人口原理論》一文中，英國經濟學家托馬斯·羅伯特·麥爾圖主張，當人口超過基本生活所需的食物供應能力時，食品價格上漲、實質工资（即以購買力衡量的工资）下降，以及貧困群體中的人均消費量下降。

By the early fourteenth century, France found itself in a classical Malthusian trap.  到 14世紀初，法國陷入了經典的麥爾圖陷阱中。

A clear indicator of increased Malthusian pressures was thirteenth-century inflation.  十三世紀通貨膨脹是增加麥爾圖壓力的明確指標。

The price of the main staple, wheat, more than doubled between 1200 and 1300.  主要糧食作物大麥的價格在 1200 年到 1300 年之間超過兩倍。

Similar price increases took place in England and Italy.  類似的價格上漲也發生在英格蘭和意大利。

Other commodities were also affected.  其他商品也被影響了。

For example, in the twelfth century, an ox could be bought in England for three shillings.  例如，在 twelfth 個世紀，在英格蘭可以以三英鎊購買一頭牛。

By the beginning of the thirteenth century, the average price was already six shillings.  到 13世紀初，平均價格已經達到六英鎊。

Toward the end of the century, the price of oxen doubled yet again.  在該世紀末，牛價又翻倍了。
