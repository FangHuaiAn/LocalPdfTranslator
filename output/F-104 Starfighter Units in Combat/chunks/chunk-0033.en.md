During the Cuba affair the F-104s were selected partly because, in
addition to Il-28 bombers and numerous nuclear-tipped missiles, Soviet
MiG-21s had been sighted on the island. From more than 500 tactical
fighter and fighter-bomber aircraft poised to defend the southern areas of
the USA, including F-105 Thunderchiefs and F-4 Phantom IIs, the F-104
was thought most capable of meeting and defeating these opponents.
