CHINA CRISIS
Within months of establishing operational readiness, the 83rd FIS was
tasked with peacekeeping in a potential international conflict thousands
of miles from its California home. On 23 August 1958 the army of the
People’s Republic of China (PRC) began a devastating artillery assault on
the disputed offshore islands of Quemoy and Matsu – up to 450,000
shells were fired. The islands had been occupied by the Nationalist
Chinese after the civil war that had brought about their Chinese People’s
Republic, centred on the island of Formosa (later called Taiwan).
Artillery duels between mainland communist Chinese forces and those
on Quemoy had continued since 1954, but the 1958 tensions included
aerial battles between the Chinese Nationalist Air Force (CNAF, later
Republic of China Air Force) and communist Chinese MiGs, 200 of which
had been moved to two airfields on the mainland opposite Taiwan.
Overflights of Quemoy by MiG-17s began on 18 August. On 24 September
a group of experienced CNAF F-86F Sabre pilots claimed the destruction
of 11 out of a wave of 100 MiG-15s and MiG-17s, including four shot
down with AIM-9B Sidewinders – the first combat use of this missile.
Within six weeks they had claimed 31 MiGs for the loss of only two Sabres.
To demonstrate support for the new republic, substantial US forces
were deployed to Formosa, including F-100D, F-101C and B-57B attack
aircraft together with US Marine Corps F4D Skyrays and US Navy
Seventh Fleet carriers offshore. The early arrivals were expected to provide
air defence for Taiwan, and the 83rd FIS was a late addition to the original
