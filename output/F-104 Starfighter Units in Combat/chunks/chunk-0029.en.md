In order to maintain mission readiness for any likely action there was
considerable air-to-air combat practice flown between the F-104C flights.
TAC also required the deployed squadrons to maintain currency in
gunnery and ordnance delivery, so they spent time on the Zaragosa range.
And when the 65th AD F-86D unit at Torrejon AB converted to the
F-102A Delta Dagger, the 436th FIS also had to provide an alert flight at
that Spanish base. The 479th TFW’s Cold War deployments to Morón
continued until 1963.
The 479th also shared alert status with ANG F-104As by transferring
squadrons to Hahn and Ramstein air bases during the Berlin Wall Crisis.
The prospect of actual conflict was seen as a real one, exemplified by the
fact that 435th TFS F-104Cs that staged through Morón in September
1961 were armed at the end of the runway prior to flying on to Ramstein.
Pilots had been warned that their arrival might trigger a response from
the East German MiGs that were based a few miles across the border from
them. George Wells, who had recently transitioned to the F-104C, joined
the 435th TFS deployment;
‘When we arrived at Ramstein an air force colonel from the area
headquarters gave us a briefing on the current state of the conflict. We
were told that the Russians were threatening to shoot down some of the
cargo aeroplanes that were supplying Berlin. He added that if they were
to go so far as to down one of our air force aeroplanes we were going to
go in and “shoot the bastards down”.’
As a further demonstration on the wing’s combat capability, the 479th
TFW won the 1962 William Tell Fighter Weapons competition. Capt
Charles Tofferi, alternately flying F-104Cs 57-0914 and 57-0915, beat
14 F-100 and F-105D pilots in conventional and nuclear delivery and in
gunnery. He attained perfect scores (3000 points) in air-to-air gunnery,
air-to-ground rocketry, strafing and napalm delivery, and drew
considerable publicity to the F-104’s weapons capability. Tofferi would
subsequently be killed in action over Laos in October 1966. 33
