Bhatti, with Sqn Ldr Amjad Hussein in 56-0804, was obliged to return
to Amritsar later on 4 December after this aborted attack, where they were
once again met by IAF aircraft. Two Su-7 ‘Fitters’ appeared ahead of them,
and as they turned to engage the Starfighters Amjad managed to get onto
the tail of one of them, while the second Su-7 positioned itself behind him.
To complete the chase, Bhatti closed up behind the second Sukhoi and
called for Amjad to ‘break right’. This enabled Bhatti to set up and fire an
AIM-9B from 56-0879, and he claimed that the IAF fighter was hit and
crashed – one of seven Su-7s destroyed in the day’s fighting. Bhatti then
accelerated ahead for a shot at the lead ‘Fitter’ and fired his second AIM-9B,
but he did not see any result as he was simultaneously attempting to jettison
his underwing fuel tanks. Only one tank released, throwing the Starfighter
into a dangerous asymmetric roll at around 600 knots. Bhatti was able to
climb, shed some speed and restore control for a safe recovery at Sargodha.
The Amritsar radar was still operational, so a fourth attack was
organised. Sqn Ldr Amjad Hussain led the strike once again, with Flt Lt
Samad Changezi as his wingman. On his first strafing pass Amjad was
distracted by a decoy radar van, which he destroyed with 20 mm fire
before seeing the real antenna operating nearby. Both pilots reefed their
jets around for a second pass and took out the radar with gunfire, although
Hussain’s 56-0804 was hit by a burst of AAA from the 27th Air Defence
Regiment. The long-suffering Starfighter began to burn and Amjad
Hussain was forced to eject. He duly became a prisoner-of-war, and had
the dubious distinction of being the only F-104A pilot to be shot down
in both the 1965 and 1971 conflicts.
