The 435th TFS’s battle with SAMs was not over as, on at least one
occasion, they were called in to bomb a SAM site. This was a challenging
task for an aircraft that Maj Gen Stan Hood conceded ‘was not an air-to-
ground aeroplane – it was a tremendous high-altitude interceptor’. Former
F-104 instructor pilot Lt Gen Tom Baker added that it was ‘low on
combat loads and on-station time’.
On one attack in October 1966 Maj Rock saw two sets of M117s ‘hit at
least a mile from the target’. Without precision bombing gear in such a
high-threat environment the F-104Cs were clearly at a disadvantage.
Mainly, it was obvious that they required ECM protection if they were to
continue operations over North Vietnam, or areas where radar-directed
AAA might be encountered. Project Pronto equipped F-104Cs with the
Applied Technologies AN/APR-25 radar warning receiver and AN/APR-26
launch warning receiver, similar to the ECM devices in F-105s, F-4s and
some F-100s.
