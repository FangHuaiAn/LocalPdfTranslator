In due course the RoCAF obtained a variety of surplus Starfighters
including F-104Ds, RF-104Gs and, in 1987, 31 ex-Japan Air Self-Defense
Force F-104Js and five F-104DJs (with another ten time-expired airframes
for spare parts), bringing the total used to at least 217 aircraft. Their
arrival allowed the RoCAF to withdraw earlier versions from service by
1983. AIM-9J Sidewinders and Chung Shan Institute Sky Sword 1 all-
aspect infrared missiles (similar to the AIM-9G Sidewinder) were available
as armament upgrades. The largest batch of ‘pre-owned’ aircraft came in
the form of 39 ex-Luftwaffe F-104Gs (including 11 modified as RF-104Gs
and 27 TF-104Gs), delivered from 1984 onwards. Another 18 F-104Gs
and TF-104Gs were added from Royal Danish Air Force surplus stock
from 1988.
Attrition of the earlier Lockheed/Canadair-supplied aircraft had been
high, with 58 aircraft allegedly lost in accidents. Six squadrons plus a
reconnaissance unit remained on strength in 1990, with the intention of
operating them until 1996. In 1993 the 427th TFW began to retire its
aircraft, followed by the 499th TFW in 1997. On 22 May 1998, 38 years
of service (totalling 380,000 flying hours) by RoCAF F-104s ended at
Ching Chuan Kang AB with the disbandment of the 12th TRS. Several
aircraft received special tail markings at this time to commemorate the
eight units that had flown the aircraft and maintained RoCAF air
superiority against a far larger communist threat.
