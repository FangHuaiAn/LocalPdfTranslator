The 538th FIS became the third ADC F-104A unit in May 1958,
trading up from F-86L Sabres at Larson AFB, Washington. The final unit
was the 56th FIS at Wright-Patterson AFB, which acquired F-104As in
July 1958. Deliveries of the remaining examples of the 153 F-104As
assigned to ADC continued until December of that year.
STATE MILITIA STARFIGHTERS
This brief ‘stop gap’ period of ADC service ended with the phase out of its
F-104As and their transfer to Air National Guard (ANG) squadrons.
However, ADC still had access to them as ADC-gained units, and this made
them frontline squadrons in an emergency, ready to deploy abroad if
necessary. Westover AFB’s aircraft began to transfer to Knoxville for service
with the 151st FIS/134th FG, Tennessee ANG, under the command of Lt
Col Robert Akin on 26 May 1960. By 1 July that year the unit had completed
its conversion from the F-86L and thus become an ADC-gained unit.
At Congaree AB, South Carolina, the 157th FIS/169th FG (South
Carolina ANG) also relinquished F-86Ls in favour of F-104A/Bs from
February 1960, as did the 197th FIS/161st FIG of the Arizona ANG at
Skyharbour Airport, Phoenix, in July 1960. The latter unit was manned
by no fewer than 750 personnel and equipped with 22 F-104As, some of
its aircraft coming from the disbanded 538th FIS.
From the original ADC allocation 24 F-104As were converted into
QF-104 target drones for the 3205th Drone Squadron and three were
passed to NASA and converted into NF-104As with Rocketdyne AR-2
rocket engines for training astronauts. Maj Ken D Luedeke was a member
of the initial QF-104 test team at Eglin AFB, Florida, from 9 January
1961, developing an effective aerial target to train fighter pilots, many of
whom would eventually meet real MiGs over North Vietnam;
