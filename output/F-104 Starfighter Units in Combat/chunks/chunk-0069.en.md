With only half of the original force of 14 F-104s airworthy by 1970,
No 9 Sqn pilots had to supplement their flying time with sorties in F-86Fs
and Shenyang J-6s as spares levels for the remaining Starfighters ran down
and they were progressively placed in storage. However, assistance
eventually reached the PAF via another F-104A/B user, the Royal
Jordanian Air Force (RJAF).
Jordan requested 32 ex-RoCAF F-104As and four F-104Bs in 1966.
The two-seat jets and a pair of F-104As, along with a USAF training
detachment, reached Prince Hassan Air Base (H-5) in June 1967 just as
the Arab-Israeli Six-Day War broke out. America then interrupted
deliveries and withdrew the H-5 aircraft to Turkey, while 18 RJAF pilots
who were training on the type in the USA with the USAF’s 4760th
Combat Crew Training Wing (which had formed with the personnel and
aircraft of the 331st FIS and led by Howard Kidwell) returned to Jordan
in October to fly Hunters instead.
By then the majority of Jordan’s other combat aircraft had been
destroyed at their bases by Israeli air strikes. F-104 deliveries resumed in
1969, and conversion training recommenced in the USA. Only 22
F-104As were eventually supplied to No 9 Sqn at H-5, including five that
had been returned to the USA following service with the RoCAF.
In 1968, in anticipation of their arrival, the RJAF asked Pakistan for
assistance with interim training, and No 9 Sqn was operational by September
1969. This relationship proved mutually beneficial when Jordan loaned some
of its Starfighters to the PAF during the second Indo-Pakistani conflict,
which began with the civil war between West and East Pakistan following
the latter’s declaration of independence as Bangladesh in March 1971. 81
