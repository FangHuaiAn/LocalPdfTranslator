In April 1965 George Wells joined the 476th TFS on its first F-104
deployment to Southeast Asia as part of Operation Two Buck. He had to
organise tankers for 18 fighters on one of the longest over-water flights
ever made by TAC aircraft, via Hickam AFB, Hawaii, with seven in-flight
refuelling hook-ups. They staged through Anderson AFB, Guam, before
finally reaching their main operating and maintenance base at Kung
Kuan, in Taiwan. Four aircraft had to abort en route with mechanical
problems. Once in Taiwan following the four-day transit, the squadron
awaited further orders. These arrived on 19 April when 14 F-104Cs
proceeded to Da Nang AB and flew escort sorties the following day.
One of the unit’s first missions foreshadowed many that the 479th
TFW would fly during two follow-on deployments to Southeast Asia.
